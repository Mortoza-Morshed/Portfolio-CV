I build end-to-end products that solve real problems - from architecting backends to crafting pixel-perfect frontends.

There's a version of every software product that looks great in a demo — until a real user touches it. Payments fail silently. Sessions expire mid-checkout. Inventory goes negative. Nobody catches it until it's someone's problem.

I decided a long time ago that I wasn't going to be the developer who ships that version. So I started building the systems that actually have to work — with real money moving through them, real roles enforcing access, and real consequences if something breaks.
